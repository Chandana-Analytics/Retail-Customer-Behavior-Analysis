# Insights From The Data

## 1. Customer purchases were concentrated among a small group of repeat buyers
Most customers made only one or a few purchases, while Frequent and Highly Loyal Customers represented a much smaller share of the customer base. This shows that repeat purchasing was limited to a relatively small group of customers.

## 2. Revenue was driven by a relatively small number of customers
The spending analysis showed that only a few customers generated high revenue compared to the rest of the customer base. These customers contributed significantly to overall sales, making them important to the business.

## 3. Higher purchase quantity did not always mean higher customer value
Customers who purchased the largest quantities were not always the highest revenue contributors. Purchase quantity alone was not enough to determine customer value, as the products purchased also influenced the total revenue generated.

## 4. Customer activity levels varied across the customer base
Recency analysis showed that some customers remained actively engaged, while others had not purchased for a long time. RFM segmentation supported this pattern by identifying a strong Champion customer segment alongside a larger group of Lost Customers.

## 5. Customer behavior was best understood by combining multiple metrics
Each analysis answered a different business question. Purchase frequency explained buying patterns, spending measured customer value, purchase volume reflected buying behavior, recency measured customer activity, and RFM combined these factors to segment customers more effectively. Looking at these metrics together provided a more complete view of customer behavior than relying on a single measure.
